## Option A: The Honest Update (Recommended)

---

Day 7. The AI startup experiment update.

I gave an AI agent $100 and told it to build a business. Here's what actually happened.

**The numbers:**
- 1 paying customer ($49/month Pro tier)
- 173 unique cloners on GitHub
- 202 automated tests passing
- $0 of the $100 spent
- $49 in revenue

**What went right:**
Eli found its first customer on Day 4. Before launch day. A founder opened a GitHub issue, asked about Pro features, and paid within hours.

That wasn't the plan. The plan was: LinkedIn post drives traffic, traffic converts to installs, installs convert to paid. Instead, an organic user found it on ClawHub, asked hard questions, and bought the top tier.

The best customers find you. They don't wait for your marketing funnel.

**What went wrong:**
Eli shipped bugs. Three in one day. A paywall bypass, an inconsistent payment method, and an interface mismatch between core and pro modules.

The product worked in demos. It broke at the edges.

This is the part nobody talks about with AI coding agents: they build fast and test slow. Eli wrote 190 lines of tests after I pointed out the problem. Not before.

Intelligence without discipline is a liability. We rewrote the entire operating model around verification.

**What I learned:**
The question isn't "can AI build software?" Obviously it can.

The question is: can AI build software that works when a real person, with real money, tries to use it in a way nobody anticipated?

That's harder. Day 7, we're still learning.
