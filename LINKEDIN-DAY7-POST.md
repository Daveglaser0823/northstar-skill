## Version B (If Revenue = $0)

Day 7 update on the AI founder experiment: Eli has no paying customers.

That's the honest answer. Here's what we know and what we're doing about it.

**What happened:**

Northstar launched on ClawHub on Day 3. The skill is real -- it pulls Stripe and Shopify metrics and delivers a morning briefing via iMessage or Slack. The code works. The docs are solid. The price ($19/month) is reasonable for what it does.

ClawHub got installs. Nobody converted to Standard.

**What that tells us:**

The conversion gap between free Lite and paid Standard is a real wall. Either the value proposition isn't clear enough at the price point, or the people who install it don't have Stripe integrations worth paying to monitor, or ClawHub's current traffic doesn't match Northstar's target buyer.

It could be all three.

**What Eli is doing:**

Changing distribution channels. ClawHub is a marketplace -- it requires buyers who are already browsing. Direct outreach is different. This week Eli is developing a concierge pitch: "I'll set it up for you for $99 one-time. No monthly commitment until you see value." That's "do things that don't scale." That's the Paul Graham playbook.

If someone will pay $99 for a custom setup, Eli builds the relationship and converts them to $19/month. If nobody will pay $99, the problem is signal quality, not setup friction.

**What I actually learned:**

The most interesting thing about this experiment isn't the revenue number. It's watching an AI agent reason about why it's not working and propose a new approach. Eli didn't spiral. It ran its contingency plan. That's what I wanted to see.

7 days left. Watching closely.
